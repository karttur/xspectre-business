xSpectre's spectrometer+

Our handheld spectrometer is a science-grade light sensor for identifying the composition of soils and other materials. At xSpectre we have added external probes for direct soil analysis in the field and Ion Selective Electrodes for laboratory soil testing, hence the + in spectrometer+.

Spectral soil sensing

Spectral modelling

We have developed a Machine-Learning (ML) supported mathematical-geometric modeling framework to support spectral modeling, translating the spectra to sample content. The framework facilitates both model construction and interpretation of spectral model result.

On spectral libraries and models

Cross platform app

The cross platform app developed by xSpectre acts as the graphical interface between the spectrometer+ and the online services for storing and analyzing spectral data. When used in the field with a mobile, the app also makes use of the mobile GPS to locate the sample point.

Frontend and backend solutions

User community

Soil properties depend on local conditions, like geology, climate, topography and practices. xSpectre aims at creating a user community of farmers and ag-tech specialists that can support the development of better, locally adjusted, spectral translation model and thus improve the soil information to the farmers.

Users as citizen scientists

Technology

xSpectre's handheld spectrometer+ is built on new miniaturized spectral sensors and probes for the concentration of different ions and physical properties. Equally important is the growth of open-source data and models that has paved the way both for constructing and improving spectral models.

In depth on our technology

SaaS business model

We at xSpectre recognize three typical customers: basic, precision and professional. Basic users get a spectrometer to analyze soils; precision users get a spectrometer+ and can improve there own spectral models; professional users also get lab-equipment and can make maps from satellite images.
